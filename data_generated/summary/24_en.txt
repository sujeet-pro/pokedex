Arbok is the Cobra Pokémon, a pure Poison-type species introduced in the first generation of Pokémon. It is a large serpentine creature, predominantly deep purple along its body, with a broad, flared hood and a cream-colored underbelly bearing a pattern of fierce, face-like markings. These hood markings are perhaps its most defining feature: when Arbok spreads its neck wide in a threat display, the pattern on its underside presents what appears to be a ferocious grimacing visage, designed to startle and unsettle any who face it. Arbok is substantially larger than a human adult, measuring roughly three and a half meters from snout to tail tip and carrying considerable muscular mass along its coiling frame. Its tail ends in a fine point, and its tongue is deeply forked, flickering constantly to sample the surrounding air for traces of heat and scent.

Arbok inhabits grassland environments across a wide range of regions, preferring open plains, sun-warmed meadows, and areas with tall vegetation where its long body can move with ease. It has been recorded in subtropical and tropical climates more frequently than in colder zones, though it has adapted to a surprising range of temperatures given how widespread its documented range truly is. Wild Arbok are predominantly solitary, claiming and defending individual territories rather than forming social groups. They are most active during the evening and into the night, when their heat-sensitive tongue gives them a clear advantage in tracking warm-bodied prey. During daylight hours, Arbok often rests coiled beneath rocky outcroppings or within dense brush, conserving energy before the hunt.

Arbok is a dedicated carnivore, relying on a combination of constriction and venom to subdue its prey. It typically locates a target by sensing heat and movement, then strikes with speed before wrapping its powerful coils around the prey to immobilize it completely. Its venom is potent and fast-acting once delivered, and experienced Arbok employ both constriction and a venomous bite as a layered hunting strategy. In terms of temperament, wild Arbok are notoriously territorial and can be deeply aggressive toward anything that enters their space uninvited. Researchers who have observed them in the field report that Arbok possesses a persistent memory for perceived threats and will follow an antagonist across a considerable distance before giving up pursuit. Arbok raised from Ekans under the care of a patient trainer, however, often develop intense loyalty and a calmer, more measured disposition over time.

In battle, Arbok leans toward physical offense, and its most impactful ability on entry is Intimidate, which immediately reduces the opposing Pokémon's physical attack capability the moment Arbok steps onto the field. This makes it a strong and disruptive switch-in against physically oriented threats, forcing opponents to recalculate their approach. Its secondary standard ability, Shed Skin, reflects its biological tendency to molt: there is a meaningful chance each turn that Arbok sloughs off whatever major status ailment it is carrying, whether that is paralysis, poison, or sleep, giving it notable resilience in prolonged engagements. The hidden ability Unnerve prevents any opposing Pokémon from consuming held Berries while Arbok is present, which can deny recovery items at a crucial moment. As a Poison-type, Arbok resists fairy, grass, fighting, and bug moves, though it must be cautious around Ground-type and Psychic-type attackers, which represent its most significant vulnerabilities.

Arbok is the evolved form of Ekans, the smaller serpent that precedes it in the Pokédex, reaching this stage through accumulated battle experience and growth. Arbok does not evolve further in the main Pokémon series, making it the final and fully realized form of its evolutionary line. It holds a notable place in Pokémon history as a signature species associated with Team Rocket's early leadership, which gave it a cultural identity tied to intimidation and cunning strategy. Beyond popular recognition, Arbok is of genuine interest to naturalists because its hood markings vary by region, a phenomenon representing one of the earliest and clearest examples of intraspecies visual diversity documented in the Pokédex. Researchers continue to record the variants and investigate whether each pattern serves a distinct ecological purpose in its respective habitat. For trainers, Arbok remains a reliable and characterful partner whose combination of disruptive presence, physical power, and resilience makes it a worthwhile choice at any stage of a journey.
