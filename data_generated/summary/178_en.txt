Xatu is known as the Mystic Pokémon, a dual Psychic and Flying type that first appeared in the Johto region during the second generation of discoveries. It stands roughly the height of a young child at about one and a half meters tall, with an upright, slender avian body draped in deep green plumage. Its wings bear striking patterns of red and yellow markings arranged in horizontal bands that strongly resemble the carved imagery found on ancient totem poles, a visual detail that carries unmistakable cultural gravity. Xatu's face is distinguished by sharp, forward-facing eyes set on a narrow head, and researchers widely note that one eye is said to peer into the distant past while the other gazes into the imminent future. Its most defining behavioral trait is a trademark stillness: Xatu is commonly observed standing completely motionless for hours on end, often oriented in the direction of the rising sun, as though conducting a silent vigil at the edge of time itself.

Xatu inhabits forested areas, typically in regions with dense old-growth canopy and relatively low human disturbance. It prefers locations where tall trees offer both elevated perching sites and a degree of natural seclusion, and it gravitates toward the interior of forests rather than their margins. Within Johto, sightings are most frequently reported in remote wilderness corridors and the deep forest zones surrounding protected parklands. Xatu is not a migratory species in the conventional sense, though individual specimens may relocate seasonally when food resources shift or when human encroachment disturbs their territory. The population of Xatu in any given forest is invariably sparse; it is a thoroughly solitary Pokémon and does not form flocks or colonies. Trainers and field researchers who encounter Xatu in the wild often remark on how easy it is to walk past one without noticing it, because its complete stillness causes it to blend into dappled forest shadow with remarkable effectiveness.

Xatu feeds primarily on seeds, berries, and small insects, foraging with methodical patience during the early morning hours before settling into its characteristic motionless stance for the remainder of the day. The Pokémon is largely diurnal, rousing around dawn and positioning itself to face eastward as the sun rises above the canopy. Field researchers universally note the unsettling quality of its stillness: unlike most avian Pokémon, Xatu does not fidget, shift its weight, or blink with any regularity. The prevailing explanation, supported by psychic researchers, is that Xatu perceives events from the distant past and the imminent future simultaneously, and that the cognitive burden of processing both timelines at once produces the outward appearance of paralysis. Toward humans, Xatu is neither aggressive nor particularly approachable; it seems simply indifferent, as though the preoccupations of the present moment are of little interest to a creature that already knows what is coming. In Johto folklore, Xatu is regarded as a sacred oracle bird, and some communities historically interpreted its motionless vigil as a form of prophecy.

In battle, Xatu draws on three possible abilities. Synchronize causes any burn, paralysis, or poison inflicted upon Xatu to be reflected directly back onto the attacker, which makes opponents genuinely cautious about using status-inflicting moves against it. Early Bird allows Xatu to recover from sleep in half the usual time, blunting the effectiveness of strategies that rely on putting it to sleep as a means of control. Its hidden ability, Magic Bounce, is particularly formidable: it reflects most non-damaging moves straight back at the user, effectively punishing opponents who attempt to set entry hazards, inflict status conditions, or lower Xatu's stats without attacking directly. As a Psychic and Flying type, Xatu is entirely immune to Ground-type attacks and resists Fighting-type moves, but it is notably vulnerable to Bug, Ghost, Dark, Rock, Electric, and Ice-type attacks, giving opponents several viable angles of approach. Its special attack and speed are its strongest qualities, positioning it best in a support and disruption role rather than a straightforwardly offensive one. Trainers who leverage Magic Bounce find Xatu especially valuable against teams that lean heavily on status and hazard strategies.

Xatu is the final form in a two-stage evolutionary line, evolving from Natu once Natu reaches level twenty-five. Natu is a small, compact, nearly flightless bird with the same characteristic green colouring and latent psychic abilities, but it lacks the full wingspan and the elaborate totem-like wing markings that define its evolved form. The progression from Natu to Xatu represents a significant physical and psychic maturation: the wings develop fully, the psychic sight sharpens to encompass both temporal directions at once, and the creature assumes the tall, solemn posture that has made it visually iconic across generations of trainers. Within the broader Pokédex, Xatu occupies a genuinely singular niche as one of the very few Pokémon whose central lore revolves around temporal perception rather than elemental power or physical prowess. Researchers in psychic Pokémon biology study it extensively in hopes of understanding how a living organism can process non-linear time without losing cognitive function entirely. Its design, rooted in the visual language of totem poles and ancient Mesoamerican bird symbolism, gives it a cultural distinctiveness that few Pokémon of its generation match.
